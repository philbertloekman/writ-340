Takeoff
Normal departure Flaps retracted, initial climb underway. LA A faulty AOA sensor from the prior flight was not corrected. ET Crew had no knowledge of the Lion Air crash five months earlier.
Sensor Fault
Faulty AOA sensor triggers a false stall warning LA 20+ degree sensor disagreement. ET 59.2 degree disagreement within seconds of takeoff. No cockpit alert existed to warn the crew.
MCAS
Activates
Automatic nose-down command of 2.5 degrees per activation The stick-shaker fires and pilots feel a sudden nose-down push. MCAS was not documented in the flight manual and neither crew had been trained on it.
Crew
Response
Crew pulls back and applies electric trim to counter the nose-down push Each input recovers about 0.4 degrees. MCAS resets and fires again 5 seconds later, removing 2.5 degrees. ET Briefly cut stabilizer trim but re-engaged it at high speed, making manual trim physically impossible.
